### 2.4 Brother (Abroad) — Remote Contributor

| Detail           | Description                                                     |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Location         | Outside Canada                                                  |
| Involvement      | Remote contractor or content contributor                        |
| Start            | Q3 2026 (after business is established)                         |

**How to Involve Legally and Effectively:**

#### Option A: Independent Contractor (Recommended)

| Detail           | Description                                                     |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Arrangement      | Contractor agreement (written, signed)                          |
| Payment          | Per project or per hour via PayPal / Wise / bank transfer       |
| Tax Impact (You) | 100% deductible as "subcontractor" expense                      |
| Tax Impact (Him) | He reports income in his country of residence                   |
| CRA Requirement  | No T4 needed for non-resident contractors                       |

**Tasks He Could Perform:**

- Product research and supplier sourcing
- Social media content creation (from abroad)
- Customer service (different timezone = extended hours)
- Translation services (if applicable)
- Video editing for TikTok/Instagram ads
- AI platform testing
- Cookie recipe research and market research
- Graphic design work

**Documentation Required:**

- Written contractor agreement (scope, rate, payment terms)
- Invoices from him for each payment
- Payment confirmations (PayPal/Wise receipts)
- Record of work delivered

#### Option B: Profit-Sharing Partner (Future, Post-Incorporation)

- After incorporation, you could add him as a shareholder or profit-share partner
- More complex, involves international tax considerations
- Consult a tax professional before pursuing this

#### Important Tax Considerations

| Item                           | Rule                                                |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Payments to non-residents      | Generally deductible as business expense             |
| Withholding tax                | May apply depending on treaty with his country       |
| Transfer pricing               | Pay fair market rate (don't overpay to shift income) |
| Documentation                  | Keep contractor agreement, invoices, payment proof   |
