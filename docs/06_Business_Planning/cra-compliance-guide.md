## 1. Why This Matters

CRA can audit sole proprietors up to 6 years after a filing date. To defend your deductions, you
need **contemporaneous documentation** — records created at or near the time of the activity.

This guide describes exactly what to keep, how to organize it, and how to be audit-ready.

## 3. What CRA Looks For ("Reasonable Expectation of Profit")

CRA determines if your business is legitimate based on these factors. Your business satisfies all
of them:

| CRA Factor                         | Your Evidence                                          |
|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Profit and loss history            | Year 1-2 loss is normal; growth plan documented         |
| Taxpayer's training/experience     | IT professional with technical expertise                |
| Intended course of action          | Documented 5-year plan with milestones                  |
| Capability to show a profit        | Multiple revenue streams, scalable SaaS product         |
| Nature of the expenses             | All directly related to business operations             |
| Business plan                      | Complete business plan with revenue targets              |
| Time spent on the activity         | Documented activity logs (10-20 hrs/week)               |
| Personal motivation                | Business purpose, not hobby                             |

### CRA Narrative Statement (Keep With Your Records)

> "My business, operated under the trade name Bornara AI, began in 2025 as a sole proprietorship.
> In 2026, the business focused on two primary revenue streams: Bornara Tools (a free ad-supported
> online toolbox at tools.bornara.com) and a cookie baking business operated by my wife. Technology
> consulting was pursued on an opportunistic basis. Two additional streams (Giftifye.com e-commerce
> and an AI SaaS platform) were strategically deferred to 2027 to focus limited time and resources
> on executing the two core streams well. The business generated revenue of $[X] in 2026 against
> startup expenses totaling $[X], resulting in a net loss of $[X]. This loss is normal and expected
> for a business in its first full year of operations. The business has a documented 5-year plan,
> real customers, active marketing, and a clear path to profitability. All expenses are directly
> related to business operations and are supported by receipts, timesheets, and activity logs."

## 4. Documentation Templates

### 4.1 Business Activity Log (Weekly)

Record this weekly at minimum:

```text
Date:       2026-XX-XX
Project:    [Giftifye / AI Platform / Consulting / Cookies]
Tasks:      [List specific tasks completed]
Time Spent: [X hours]
Outcome:    [What was accomplished]
Notes:      [Any relevant details]
```

### 4.2 Travel Log Entry

```text
Date:       2026-XX-XX
From:       [Start location]
To:         [Destination]
Purpose:    [Business reason]
KM Driven:  [X km]
Fuel/Parking: $[X]
```

### 4.3 Meal/Entertainment Log

```text
Date:       2026-XX-XX
Location:   [Restaurant name]
Amount:     $[X] (50% deductible = $[X])
Attendees:  [Names and business roles]
Purpose:    [Specific business discussion topic]
```

## 5. Monthly Compliance Checklist

Do this at the end of every month:

- [ ] Update income log with all revenue received
- [ ] File all expense receipts (digital or physical)
- [ ] Complete wife's timesheet and make payment
- [ ] Complete kids' timesheets if applicable
- [ ] Update business activity log
- [ ] Update travel log
- [ ] Screenshot Shopify dashboard (revenue proof)
- [ ] Save advertising receipts (Facebook, TikTok, Google)
- [ ] Save software subscription invoices
- [ ] Update expense tracking spreadsheet

---

## 6. Quarterly Review Checklist

Every 3 months:

- [ ] Review revenue vs. targets (from [12-Month Roadmap](12-month-roadmap.md))
- [ ] Review expense totals vs. budget
- [ ] Ensure all documentation is current
- [ ] Update CRA narrative if business direction changes
- [ ] Review wife/kids hours and payments for reasonableness
- [ ] Back up all digital records
- [ ] Take screenshots of all active websites (proof of business activity)

---

## 7. Year-End Checklist (December)

- [ ] Final payment to wife (ensure annual total is documented)
- [ ] Final payment to kids
- [ ] Complete home office calculation for the year
- [ ] Calculate CCA for all equipment
- [ ] Compile all T2125 figures
- [ ] Write year-end CRA narrative
- [ ] Gather all signed receipts
- [ ] Export Shopify revenue reports
- [ ] Compile advertising spend totals
- [ ] Review and finalize travel log
- [ ] Back up everything to multiple locations
- [ ] File with T1 return (Wealthsimple or accountant)

## 8. Evidence of Business Activity (Audit Proof)

|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| Website screenshots            | Monthly screenshots of Giftifye, AI platform, etc.    |
| Social media activity          | Screenshots of posts, follower growth                  |
| Email marketing                | Campaign reports, subscriber counts                    |
| Shopify analytics              | Monthly revenue/traffic reports                        |
| Customer communications        | Saved emails or chat logs                              |
| Development activity           | GitHub commit history (this repository!)               |
| Ad platform reports            | Facebook/TikTok ad manager exports                     |
| Client communications          | Consulting proposal emails, contracts                  |
| Cookie orders                  | Order records, delivery confirmations                  |

## 9. Records Retention

| Record Type           | Retention Period                                       |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Income records        | 6 years from filing date                               |
| Expense receipts      | 6 years from filing date                               |
| CCA schedules         | As long as you own the asset + 6 years                 |
| Payroll records       | 6 years from filing date                               |
| T2125 copies          | 6 years from filing date                               |
| Business activity logs| 6 years from filing date                               |
