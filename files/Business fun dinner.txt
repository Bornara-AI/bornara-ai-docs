1. Can you deduct “family fun” or “team dinners” as business expenses?
Here’s the honest, CRA‑compliant breakdown.

✅ YES — but only under specific conditions.
CRA does allow meals & entertainment deductions IF:

The meal is directly related to business, AND

The people attending are part of the business, AND

The purpose is business‑related (planning, reviewing, strategizing), AND

You keep a record of the business purpose.

✔️ This means:
You CAN deduct a dinner with your wife IF she is legitimately part of your business (she is), and the dinner is for:

Business planning

Reviewing Shopify strategy

Discussing AI platform development

Reviewing expenses

Planning marketing

Supplier discussions

Year‑end review

❗ CRA rule:
Only 50% of meals & entertainment is deductible.

This is standard for all businesses.

✔️ What you CANNOT deduct:
Pure family fun

Birthdays

Anniversaries

Vacations

Entertainment with no business purpose

✔️ What you CAN deduct:
“Business planning dinner”

“Quarterly strategy meeting”

“Shopify product review meeting”

“AI platform planning session”

“Year‑end business review dinner”

If you document the purpose, it’s legitimate.

✔️ How to document it:
In your bookkeeping spreadsheet, add:

Date:  
Location:  
Amount:  
Attendees:  
Business purpose:

Example:

“Dinner meeting with wife (business partner) to plan Q2 Shopify product strategy.”

This is 100% CRA‑safe.
